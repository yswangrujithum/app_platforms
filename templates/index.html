Apple App Store and Google Play Store Data Analysis

The purpose of this project was to observe the the behavior of weather factors as the locations get closer to the equator. We requested data from the OpenWeatherMap API and obtained all of the information needed for more than 500 cities all over the world.

After we compiled all of necessary datasets, we used Matplotlib to create various visualizations to see the trend of the weather factors in response to the location of the cities.

As expected, the temperature is affected by the location of the city. The closer the city to the 0-degree latitude, the higher the temperature. However, some data points contradicted the previous findings, so we need to look further into other influential factors. For instance, the industrialized city temperature tends to be higher than the non-industrialized city regardless of the degree of latitude.

The percent of humidity and speed of the wind are not affected by the degree of latitude. The trend of the data is steady over the degree of latitude for both percentages of humidity and wind speed.

The data points for the percent of cloudiness are scattered all over the chart. We can recognize the trend that goes across the chart, but it doesn't depict any meaningful messages as multiple lines can be detected. Therefore, we can conclude that the percent of cloudiness is not affected by the degree of latitude.

Further investigations should be conducted to support the conclusions.
